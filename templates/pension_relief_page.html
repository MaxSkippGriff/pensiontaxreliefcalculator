How relief works at £{{ '{:,}'.format(income) }}

Which tax band applies and what relief you get

{% if calc_ras.marginal_rate >= 45 %}

On a £{{ '{:,}'.format(income) }} income you are an additional-rate taxpayer paying 45% on income above £125,140. Your marginal income tax rate is {{ '{:.0f}'.format(calc_ras.marginal_rate) }}%.

Under relief at source, your provider claims 20% basic rate relief automatically. You claim the remaining {{ '{:.0f}'.format(calc_ras.marginal_rate - 20) }}% extra relief via Self Assessment — totalling £{{ '{:,.0f}'.format(calc_ras.total_relief) }} on a £{{ '{:,}'.format(contribution) }} contribution.

{% elif calc_ras.marginal_rate >= 40 %}

On a £{{ '{:,}'.format(income) }} income you are a higher-rate taxpayer paying 40% on income above £50,270{% if income > 100000 %} — your personal allowance is also reduced by the £100k taper{% endif %}. Your marginal income tax rate is {{ '{:.0f}'.format(calc_ras.marginal_rate) }}%.

Under relief at source, your provider claims 20% basic rate relief automatically from HMRC. You claim the additional 20% extra relief via Self Assessment — totalling £{{ '{:,.0f}'.format(calc_ras.total_relief) }} on a £{{ '{:,}'.format(contribution) }} contribution.

{% else %}

On a £{{ '{:,}'.format(income) }} income you are a basic-rate taxpayer paying 20% income tax. Your marginal income tax rate is {{ '{:.0f}'.format(calc_ras.marginal_rate) }}%.

Under relief at source, your provider claims the full 20% basic rate relief automatically from HMRC — no Self Assessment needed. Total relief on a £{{ '{:,}'.format(contribution) }} contribution is £{{ '{:,.0f}'.format(calc_ras.total_relief) }}.

{% endif %}

Under salary sacrifice, your gross salary is reduced by £{{ '{:,}'.format(contribution) }} before income tax and National Insurance are calculated. This saves you income tax and employee NI (saving an additional £{{ '{:,.0f}'.format(calc_ss.employee_ni_saving) }}), bringing your effective net cost down to £{{ '{:,.0f}'.format(calc_ss.effective_cost) }}. Your employer also saves NI of £{{ '{:,.0f}'.format(calc_ss.employer_ni_saving) }} — some employers pass this saving into your pension.
