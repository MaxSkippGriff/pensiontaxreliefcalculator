£{{ "{:,}".format(salary) }} salary: income tax rate and pension context

At £{{ "{:,}".format(salary) }}, your marginal income tax rate is {{ "{:.0f}".format(calc_ras.marginal_rate) }}% (England, Wales and NI, 2026/27). {% if salary > 125140 %} You pay 45% additional rate on income above £125,140. Pension contributions reduce your adjusted net income, and any contributions within the 40% band reduce your higher-rate liability. {% elif salary > 100000 %} Your income is within the personal allowance tapering zone (£100,000–£125,140). Every £2 of pension contribution reduces adjusted net income by £2, potentially restoring personal allowance at an effective 60% marginal relief rate. {% elif salary > 50270 %} You pay 40% income tax on earnings above £50,270. For relief at source pensions, you must claim the extra 20% relief (beyond the provider's automatic 20%) via Self Assessment. {% else %} You pay 20% income tax on your earnings above the personal allowance (£12,570). Your pension provider claims this 20% automatically under relief at source — no Self Assessment claim needed. {% endif %}
